\section{Introduction}
Educational institutions are increasingly turning to intelligent digital systems to manage complex operations such as class scheduling, instructor allocation, and teaching quality assurance \cite{Lv2025, Almubarak2025}. In distributed learning environments—especially those relying on remote instruction—these challenges become more complex.

TeachWise is designed as an intelligent Course Management System (CMS) that unifies operational efficiency with pedagogical intelligence. This review synthesizes research across three major domains: (1) automated scheduling and resource optimization, (2) hybrid AI-enabled teaching evaluation using prosodic analysis and machine learning, and (3) Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) frameworks for curriculum fidelity \cite{Donnelly2016, Gao2024}.

\section{Automated Scheduling and Resource Optimization}
\subsection{Complexity of Educational Timetabling (UCTP)}
Class scheduling is traditionally framed as the University Course Timetabling Problem (UCTP), a well-established NP-hard combinatorial optimization problem. The task requires assigning courses, instructors, and student groups to limited time slots while respecting hard constraints, such as preventing double-booking, and soft constraints, such as teacher preferences \cite{Sahargahi2021}. Manual scheduling often results in inconsistencies, whereas automated meta-heuristic approaches like Genetic Algorithms or Spotted Hyena Optimizer (SHO) offer scalable, near-optimal solutions \cite{Nguyen2021, Diallo2024}.

\subsection{Intelligent Scheduling for Distributed Environments}
TeachWise operates in a distributed remote-learning context with unique challenges, including multi-time-zone scheduling and fluctuating instructor availability. Recent work by Lv (2025) demonstrates that machine learning models in intelligent teaching systems can significantly improve resource utilization and student outcomes compared to manual methods \cite{Lv2025}.

\section{AI-Powered Instructor Evaluation}
\subsection{Limitations of Traditional Evaluation Methods}
Historically, teacher evaluation has relied on student surveys and manual classroom observations. These approaches suffer from subjectivity, evaluator bias, and infrequent feedback \cite{Almubarak2025}. Modern automated systems address this by identifying teaching activities (e.g., question-answer interactions) directly from audio recordings \cite{Donnelly2016, Stone2019}.

\subsection{Prosodic Analysis and Teaching Quality}
Prosody—the rhythm, pitch, stress, and intonation of speech—is a key indicator of classroom climate and teaching effectiveness. Vocal characteristics such as pitch variability and loudness correlate with instructional dimensions like energy and patience \cite{Shoukat2025, Pardo2025}. Acoustic features extracted via tools like OpenSMILE enable the training of Random Forest classifiers to predict teaching quality dimensions with high accuracy \cite{Eyben2010, Jensen2020}.

\subsection{RAG Frameworks for Curriculum Fidelity}
While acoustic features evaluate delivery, assessing content requires semantic analysis. Standard Large Language Models (LLMs) often lack grounding in specific curriculum documents. Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) addresses this by incorporating relevant lesson plans into the model's context, achieving high agreement with human evaluators in assessing whether learning objectives were met \cite{Gao2024}.

\section{Gaps in Existing Literature}
Despite maturity in scheduling and evaluation, several gaps remain:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Scheduling–Pedagogy Integration Gap:} Timetabling systems optimize logistics but rarely incorporate real-time instructor performance metrics.
    \item \textbf{Modality Isolation:} Most systems analyze text or audio in isolation, rather than a hybrid approach combining delivery and content \cite{Stone2019}.
    \item \textbf{Domain-Specific Support:} Limited work exists on automated evaluation tailored for coding education and distributed STEM instruction.
    \item \textbf{Real-time Grounding:} Traditional topic modeling lacks the curriculum-specific grounding required for precise fidelity checks \cite{Grootendorst2022}.
\end{enumerate}

\section{TeachWise’s Contribution}
TeachWise addresses these gaps through an integrated design combining operational automation with pedagogical intelligence:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Hybrid Multimodal Pipeline:} Combines machine learning on 18 prosodic features with RAG-based semantic evaluation.
    \item \textbf{PDF-Driven Curriculum Scorer:} Implements a dynamic framework that parses teaching guides directly from PDF to evaluate fidelity without manual re-entry.
    \item \textbf{Structured Evaluation Reporting:} Generates automated reports with both quantitative scores (Likert scale) and qualitative pedagogical gap identification.
    \item \textbf{Global Operational Design:} A unified CMS designed for global remote teaching at scale.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Conclusion}
While scheduling optimization and AI-based teaching evaluation have advanced considerably, they have evolved largely in isolation. TeachWise unifies these research streams to create a multimodal CMS that automates scheduling while enhancing instructional quality through objective, scalable, and grounded evaluation.
