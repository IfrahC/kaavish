\subsection{Individual Reflections}

\subsubsection{Member 1: Aina }
Developing the TeachWise evaluation pipeline was a challenging but valuable experience. One of the main difficulties was not being able to get a complete dataset from our partner school. Because of this, we had to quickly switch plans and create and label our own dataset within a short time. This taught us the importance of having backup plans and preparing for data-related issues in large projects.
On the technical side, reaching 87.0\% accuracy for Student Attention showed that the 18-feature prosodic analysis pipeline was effective. My experience as an instructor and Teaching Assistant helped me understand how audio features relate to teaching quality.
Overall, this project highlights the need for fair and objective tools to evaluate teaching quality, while also keeping ethical considerations in mind in modern digital education systems.


The development of \italic{TeachWise} was a comprehensive and hands-on learning experience, one that went further than any theoretical course work.
A significant takeaway at the forefront of the project, was our ability to design, implement and maintain a large-scale, real-world system which is an amalgamation of multiple domains.
Domains such as web development, machine learning, database management, API integration, modern frameworks and system design. Gaining exposure to a full-stack development cycle, all of this while ensuring maintainability and scalability. 
\\
\\
\indent One of the major milestones was understanding the complexities of scheduling problems and implementing heuristic-based solutions all to achieve a near-optimal performance.
Furthermore working on the ML/NLP pipeline exposed us to many advanced concepts. Concepts such as topic modelling, speech processing and multimodal analysis. Areas that initially seemed very abstract but became clearer as the project went on.
\\
\\
\indent We encountered many technical challenges along the way, such as inetgrating multiple different systen componenets to work in tandem with each other, handling workflows that worked asynchronously, and making sure the remained consistent across modules.
Having to over come these challenges resulted in us enhancing our debugging skills, strengthening our understanding of the design of the system, and it also taught us the importance of an iterative development approach.
\\
\\
\indent Team collaboration was a major factor in the successful development of this project. Work was distributed amongs the team according to individual strengths, this allowed each member to specialize whilst also contributing to the project itself. 
Overall this enabled very efficient parallel development. 
\\
\\
\indent This was mostly due to constant and consistant communication between the team members, especially during phases where the dependencies between componenets became very important. Regular meetings (Occasionally Online), progress updates and collaborative debugging/testing sessions.
This was able to help us to resolve issues quickly and to stay aligned with our goals and timelines. One particular challenge we did face however was in synchronizing timelines, specifically when the implementation of one componenet depeneded on the completion of another.
\\
\\
\indent Another major aspect we picked up was the importance of proper documnetation, version control and early testing. The project enhanced our teamwork, adaptability and structurization skills.
\\
\\
\indent Despite the intimidating stature of the project and the challenges we faced, the overall process was highly rewarding. Not only did it reinforce our technical skills but also introduced us to the importance of other factors such as project management, collaboration and real-world system development.
